    Central and Western

    It is a district which has the most educated residents with the second highest income in Hong Kong. It is also the central business district of Hong Kong. Pro-democracy camp gains 7 seats in this election.

  • Eastern
    Eastern

    Starting from 1950s, Fujianese are centalise in this district. Pro-Beijing camp has dominated the district council since 1980s. Electors are usually won by walkover because there is no other candidate.

  • Wan Chai
    Wan Chai

    It has the second most educated residents with the highest income in Hong Kong. 46.2% of the population have tertiary education qualifications. Among all districts, it is the only one without any public estate.

  • Southern
    Southern

    There are large private housing estates in the district. The eastern half of the district is semi-rural, with some of Hong Kong's most popular beaches. The western half of the district is partly residential and partly industrial.

  • Sham Shui Po
    Sham Shui Po

    It is the poorest district in Hong Kong with lowest median household income. It also contains the oldest generation among all districts.

  • Yau Tsim Mong
    Yau Tsim Mong

    One new seat is added in this district due to the population growth in Kowloon station. Pro-democracy camp gains 17 seats while the pro-beijing camp gains only 3 seats.

  • Kowloon City
    Kowloon City

    It has the highest income in Kowloon with low residential density. The majority of its population living in private sector housing, including old tenement buildings, private residential developments and low-rise villas.

  • Wong Tai Sin
    Wong Tai Sin

    It has the least educated residents with the lowest income, the oldest residents. Over 85% of the district's residents live in public housing. All seats in the district council belong to the pro-democracy camp.

  • Kwun Tong
    Kwun Tong

    3 new seats are added due to population growth. Income is below average in Hong Kong. It is the most densely populated district and one of the largest industrial areas in Hong Kong.

  • Tuen Mun
    Tuen Mun

    2 new seats are added due to newly formed estate. Pro-democracy camp gains 28 seats while the pro-beijing camp gains 3 seats only.

  • Yuen Long
    Yuen Long

    4 new seats are added due to population growth. Pro-democracy camp gains 33 seats.

  • Tsuen Wan
    Tsuen Wan

    It has the highest income in the New Territories. Pro-democracy camp gains 16 seats while the pro-beijing camp gains 2 seats. 1 seat is belonging to the neutral party.

  • Kwai Tsing
    Kwai Tsing

    Income is below average. Over 75% of the district residents live in public housing. Both light and heavy industries share substantial land in the district.

  • Islands
    Islands

    It contributes 18% of the area of Hong Kong. The Islands District consists of some twenty large and small islands which lie to the south and southwest of Hong Kong.

  • North
    North

    70% of the population lives in the public estates. Most major access points to Mainland China from Hong Kong lies in North district.

  • Tai Po
    Tai Po

    Tai Po Industrial Estate is located in the district

  • Sha Tin
    Sha Tin

    About 65% of the district's population live in public rental housing, housing under Hong Kong's Tenants Purchase Scheme, or Home Ownership Scheme.

  • Sai Kung
    Sai Kung

    It contains most of the undeveloped area in Hong Kong. There are beaches, country parks and geoparks. Tseung Kwan O, which is an urban area, is accounting for 95% of this district.
